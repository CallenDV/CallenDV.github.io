my hobbies

i have a lot. like, a lot. also, ew, i don't like how much i'm using "i". feels narcissistic.

well, there's one obvious one, and it is the reason why you're here right now. one of my favorite hobbies is web development, and just programming in general. i started a few years ago, my earliest project tracing to roughly 2021. it functions as a great outlet for my energy and thoughts. if you want to get into web development, i highly recommend w3schools and mdn dev docs.

a newer development has been my love of photography. i have always liked taking pictures of landscapes and just wherever i happened to be, but i never took it seriously until my parents gifted me a nikon d3000 about 2 years ago. since then, practically every major trip i've been on, my d3000 has been with me. if you have any tips for me or need some, i'll gladly give. also, speaking of photography, sometime eventually i'll add a section to this website where my pictures can be seen. guess i'll never be satisfied with this website.

i don't really show it on this website, but i adore art. not really painting or anything like that, just specifically pencil/monochrome sketches. if you couldn't get a hint from my photography section literally right above this, i typically only draw landscapes, sometimes animals. not really that ood with drawing people, but i have been studying anatomy in an attempt to get better. maybe just like my photos, i'll put some of my silly little doodles on here. anyways, i think my shading is pretty good? maybe? dunno.

i also play games fairly often. mostly "older" nintendo stuff, such as splatoon 1, super mario 3d world, or tomodachi life. i don't play these emulated, i always use my actual wii u and 2ds xl, unless it is like an actually old game where i don't have the console, such as smb3 or kid icarus. i still play more modern games, but you'll usually only find me on my 2ds xl or wii u, not really my switch 2. of course, i do also play pc games, such as fallout 3 and ms flight sim.

also, it isn't really a hobby, but don't forget to go on walks, go biking, really anything outside! you never notice just how nice and peaceful nature is until you're there to see it.

music

most of the music i listen to is usually lofi, jazz, or video game music. typically nintendo. almost all of these can be found on a little website i found (do note that the link i gave you has a referral that allows me to download more albums, if you do not want to, that's totally fine. here's the non-referral link: khinsider vgm). here's a little 3d album thing i made please like it

animal crossing: new leaf ost

it's really good. as you would expect, very peaceful and relaxed. great for listening when studying or doing work of any kind. highly recommend.

femtanyl's reactor album

one of my favorites. very high paced and energetic. my personal favorite from the album is dinner!

undertale ost

a cult classic. practically everyone knows it already, but it is still worth it to listen to nontheless.

splatune

yet another great one. fast, full of energy, and strangely optimistic. absolutely love the entire thing, probably my most favorite album oat! don't just get the soundtrack, get the game too. calling it fantastic is an understatement, there are no words for how good i think this game and soundtrack are.

super mario galaxy ost

this one is very good. the soundtrack being orchestrated was definitely the right choice, it fits the whole "grand"-ness of the game perfectly, and i'm all here for that. that aside, the soundtrack is still just excellent, and has a sense of variety to it that i think other mario games just don't hit as well.
